Problem description and vision of MonArch#

I’ve long searched for a tool to visualize architecture, service-topology and monitoring in an intuitive way.

An intuitive way in this case means from my point of view, visualizing the picture I have in my mind regarding service communication and data-flow.

Architecture and or service-topology refers to what I would call “domain/business logic architecture” in microservices. Microservices inherently have a lot of data flowing between them as they are all working together to solve a larger business related problem. This data-flow consists two major building blocks, asynchronous and synchronous data-flow (think REST/GRPC APIs and event sourcing), both should be included as both are equally important. What I want to capture in the word architecture are these domain-/business-logic- data-flows. Things like Edge gateways, logging topics, monitoring collection and other orthogonal concerns to business logic are not relevant.

Monitoring refers to visualize some core metrics of each service and event topic. The insights that we want to achieve are the following.

  • how large data-flows are in terms of throughput in different parts of the service-topology
  • how are the services keeping up with the events that they are consuming
  • RED metrics for each service (this is combined with event lag to get a single state of the service healthy/degraded/critical)

The main benefit of having this visualization is that it can show any loops in the architecture, bottlenecks, god-services and other issues. It is also a nice way to quickly get an overview of what services and event topics exist. This allows greater decision-making when deciding where to inject a new service and how to collect the relevant data for that service to do its job.
